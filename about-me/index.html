Who is Steven Shi...?

The intrinsic and defining aspects of my life.

February 1996

I am a newborn Chinese native, the only child of my parents; I'm told I cried a lot for no reason.

September 2000

I'm scaling the Twin Towers and exploring the new world before me, claiming my status as a first generation American.

2000-2002

I'm learning English; nearly held back a grade for slow language development, I now understand that it was not my classmates that were stupid, but it was I who simply lacked the understanding to communicate with them.

2001-2008

I spend the majority of my childhood in the food court my parents worked in. Often cashiering by mental math to expedite service, my arithmetic matures and my interest in math beyond that of a tool sparks. In working with my parents and their coworkers, I'm pushed to grasp the concept of socioeconomic inequality and the rare American childhood it provided me.

2002

My parents buy me a personal desktop (absurd right?) marking my first ownership of "modern" technology. I purchase Red Alert 2 and spend my weekends climbing the global leaderboards. It was then that my curiosity of technology and love of games began (needless to say, I evolved to the Protoss race shortly after).

2002

I test into the gifted program with math dragging my deadweight verbal scores across the line; it would be my only intellectual refuge in the years of public schooling to come.

2005

I make my first friend in the midst of an unwelcoming environment. Making that one friend easily eclipsed all failed past attempts, or as Drew Houston put so well, "Don't worry about failure, you only have to be right once."

2005

My friend (mentioned above) invites me to a youth ministry group at his church. The people there were nicer than most I thought. One even bought me a King James Bible for my scholarly benefit.

2009

As a result of continuing my attendance at youth group, I decide to become a Christian...I didn't really understood the full implications of my choice at that time, I just thought it'd be worth a try given how nice everyone in the church treated me.

September 2010

I enter highschool with the same "veni, vidi, vici" mindset I had when I first entered America, claiming my status as a first generation high school student.

2011

I join a church community called AOX. They're good people who love Jesus and others, and in them I found my first true friends and a second family.

2012

I'm approached by some random weirdo college student from Alaska (shady right?). He convinces me to take part in a ministry called YoungLife (even shadier right?) and I'm glad to have been a part of it. He also ended up becoming one of my best friends.

2014

I apply to colleges and find myself blessed to have been accepted at Brown. I graduate from highschool shortly after and look forward to a new journey in life.

2015

And what a journey Freshman year was. Looking forward to a great Sophomore year.
