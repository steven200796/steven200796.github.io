Who is Steven Shi...?

The intrinsic and defining aspects of my life

February 1996

I am a newborn Chinese native, the only child of my parents; I'm told I cried a lot for no reason.

September 2000

I'm scaling the Twin Towers and exploring the new world before me, claiming my status as a first generation American.

2000-2002

I'm learning English; nearly held back a grade for slow language development, I now understand that it was not my classmates that were stupid, but it was I who simply lacked the understanding to communicate with them.

2002

My parents buy me a personal desktop (absurd right?) marking my first ownership of "modern" technology. I purchase Red Alert 2 and spend my weekends climbing the global leaderboards. It was then that my curiosity of technology and love of games began (needless to say, I evolved to the Protoss race shortly after).

2001

I test into the gifted program with math dragging my deadweight language scores across the line; it would be my only intellectual refuge in the years of public schooling to come.

2000-2008

I spend the majority of my childhood in the food court my parents worked in. Often cashiering by mental math to expedite service, my arithmetic matures and my interest in math beyond that of a tool sparks. In working with my parents and their coworkers, I'm pushed to grasp the concept of socioeconomic inequality and the rare American childhood it provided me.

2005

I make my first friend in the midst of an unwelcoming environment. This taught me that one valuable success can and will eclipse all past failures, or as Drew Houston put so well, "Don't worry about failure, you only have to be right once."
